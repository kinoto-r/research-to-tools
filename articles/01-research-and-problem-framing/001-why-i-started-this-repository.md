# Why I Started Research to Tools

## Background

Many of the tools I have developed began with a practical question or a repetitive task.

While working with regulations, technical documents, structured data, and research workflows, I often found that existing methods were difficult to maintain, search, or reuse.

## From Research to Tools

My development process usually starts with research rather than code.

I first identify the problem, organize the information, define the requirements, and then consider whether a practical tool can improve the workflow.

This repository documents that process.

## What This Repository Covers

The articles focus on:

- identifying operational problems
- translating research findings into requirements
- making design decisions
- using AI-assisted development tools
- improving data and knowledge structures
- learning from failed approaches and redesigns

Production source code, confidential information, and client-specific details are not included.

## Closing Note

Research to Tools is a record of how questions, research, and operational challenges can be transformed into practical systems and reusable knowledge.
